Data Philosophy

  • One source of truth: "some_document_v3_final_actual_final_version(3).xlsx" makes me cry inside
  • Work smarter, not harder:
  • Automation: Automate as much as possible
  • Incremental Improvement:(Just like this page)
  • Transparancy/Openness/Sharing: Sharing is caring, no re-inventing the wheel, every one knows what others are working on
  • Collaboration: More brains = more ideas/view points = better solutions/outcomes
  • Cloud/Live Collaboration:No saving of documents on personal devices, update documents in real time, no sending of files (send links only)

Project Management

Despite no formal PM training, during my time as a Data Lead/Project Manager, I have developed the following:

  • Requirements Documents: Objectives, Background, Design, User Stories, Deliverables, Task List
  • Reflection Documents: To reflect on things that went well, and things that could be improved upon
  • Information Packs: To provide critical information to stakeholders for complex deliverables
  • Review Meetings: Hear feedback from stakeholders
  • Change Request/Improvement Lists: Record a list of requested/identified improvements to deliverables
  • Issue Resolution Documents: List any issues/blockers, their impacts, and brainstorm possible solutions
  • Report Register: List of available dashboards and information

What I work by:

  • Coding
    • Keep code DRY
    • Use of parameterisation and reusable functions/modularity
  • Dashboarding
    • Capable in Power BI, however I appreciate freedom to use code-based dashboarding tools (eg. Streamlit) for improved:
      • Efficiency: Reduction of manual and time consuming clicking/dragging
      • Flexibility: Use of python packages, customisable UI using HTML/CSS, and greater selection and customisation of visuals
      • Increased control/Reduction of errors: The logic is readable via code, not buried within menus
      • Version Control: Simply rollback rather than contend with a fire ("oops I broke the report, now I have to figure out a fix right now")
